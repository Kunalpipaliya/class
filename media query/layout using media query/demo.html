Surat

Surat is a major city located in the Indian state of Gujarat, known for its rapid industrial growth and vibrant textile industry. Often referred to as the "Diamond City of India," Surat is a global hub for diamond cutting and polishing. It is situated on the banks of the Tapi River and has a rich history dating back to ancient trade routes. Surat is one of the cleanest cities in India and is known for its efficient municipal administration. The city is famous for its street food, especially dishes like locho, khaman, and Surti sev khamani.

Mumbai

Mumbai, the capital of Maharashtra, is the financial, commercial, and entertainment hub of India. It is home to the famous Bollywood film industry, making it a hotspot for aspiring actors and filmmakers. Located on the western coast of India, Mumbai has a deep natural harbor and is one of the busiest ports in the country. The city is known for its iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. Despite its fast-paced life and crowded streets, Mumbai is celebrated for its vibrant culture, street food, and spirit of resilience.

Kutch

Kutch is a district in the state of Gujarat, known for its unique geography, culture, and handicrafts. The region is famous for the Rann of Kutch, a vast salt desert that becomes a white wonderland during the Rann Utsav. Bhuj is the main city in Kutch and serves as the cultural and administrative center of the district. Kutch is rich in traditional crafts like embroidery, bandhani, and Rogan art, made by local artisans. Despite its arid landscape, Kutch thrives with vibrant festivals, wildlife sanctuaries, and a deep-rooted heritage.
